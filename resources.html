Victorian Tenant Rights Guide

Understanding the Residential Tenancies Act

The Residential Tenancies Act 1997 (Vic) is the main law that governs the rights and responsibilities of landlords and tenants in Victoria. Recent changes to this Act have strengthened tenant protections.

Rental Agreements and Leases

Learn about the different types of rental agreements in Victoria, including fixed-term leases and periodic (month-to-month) agreements. Understand what should be included in your agreement and your rights regarding lease renewals.

Standard Rental Agreement Condition Report Guide

Repairs and Maintenance

Landlords in Victoria are required to ensure rental properties are maintained in good repair. Learn about urgent and non-urgent repairs, how to request them, and what to do if your landlord doesn't respond.

Repairs Request Template VCAT Application Guide

Rent Increases and Bond

Victorian law regulates how often and by how much your rent can be increased. Learn about the rules for rent increases, bond payments, and how to get your bond back at the end of your tenancy.

Residential Tenancies Bond Authority Rent Increase Calculator

Financial Assistance Programs

Centrelink Rent Assistance

Commonwealth Rent Assistance is a payment that helps people who pay rent and receive a Centrelink payment. Learn about eligibility requirements, payment rates, and how to apply.

Services Australia Payment Calculator

Victorian Housing Register

The Victorian Housing Register is a common waiting list for people who want to apply for public and community housing in Victoria. Learn about priority categories and how to apply.

Housing.vic.gov.au Application Guide

Bond Loan Scheme

The Victorian Government offers interest-free bond loans to eligible low-income earners who can't afford to pay the bond for a new rental property. Learn about eligibility and how to apply.

Bond Loan Application Eligibility Checker

Emergency Housing Support

If you're at risk of homelessness or experiencing a housing crisis, there are emergency support services available in Melbourne and across Victoria.

Understanding Public Housing

Public housing in Victoria is managed by the Department of Families, Fairness and Housing. Learn about the different types of public housing available, eligibility requirements, and the application process.

Public Housing Information Property Locations

Community Housing Options

Community housing is provided by not-for-profit organizations that receive some government funding. These organizations offer various housing options, including long-term rental housing and transitional housing.

The Victorian Housing Register has priority access categories for people who need housing urgently, including those experiencing homelessness, family violence, or significant hardship.
