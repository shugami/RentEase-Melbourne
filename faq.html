Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to common questions about renting in Melbourne

What documents do I need to apply for a rental property?

To apply for a rental property in Melbourne, you typically need:

  • Photo ID (driver's license, passport)
  • Proof of income (payslips, employment contract)
  • Rental history and references
  • Bank statements
  • Centrelink statements (if applicable)
  • Character references

International students may also need to provide:

  • Student visa documentation
  • Confirmation of enrollment
  • Proof of funds or sponsorship

How much bond do I need to pay?

In Victoria, the bond amount is typically:

  • One month's rent for properties under $900 per week
  • Up to six weeks' rent for properties over $900 per week

The bond must be lodged with the Residential Tenancies Bond Authority (RTBA) within 10 business days.

What maintenance am I responsible for as a tenant?

As a tenant, you are responsible for:

  • Keeping the property clean and tidy
  • Garden maintenance (if specified in lease)
  • Replacing light bulbs
  • Basic pest control (e.g., insects)
  • Reporting maintenance issues promptly

The landlord is responsible for ensuring the property is in good repair and maintaining essential services.

What should I do before moving out?

Before moving out, ensure you:

  • Give appropriate notice (usually 28 days)
  • Clean the property thoroughly
  • Complete any repairs
  • Take photos for documentation
  • Arrange a final inspection
  • Return all keys
  • Update your address
  • Disconnect utilities

What are my rights regarding rent increases?

In Victoria, rent increases are regulated:

  • Rent can only be increased once every 12 months
  • 60 days written notice is required
  • The increase must be reasonable
  • You can challenge excessive increases through VCAT

Contact Consumer Affairs Victoria for more information about your rights regarding rent increases.
